Egusi Soup

Description

Egusi soup is a thick and savory Nigerian dish prepared with ground melon seeds, palm oil, leafy greens, and a variety of meats or fish. The soup’s nutty flavor and rich texture make it one of the most popular dishes in West African cuisine.

Traditionally served with pounded yam, fufu, or eba, egusi soup is a comfort food that warms the soul. Its combination of seeds, vegetables, and proteins creates a nourishing and filling meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 2 cups assorted meat (beef, goat, or chicken)
  • 1 cup stockfish or dried fish (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 cups leafy greens (spinach, bitterleaf, or ugu)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cups meat stock or water
  • Salt and seasoning cubes to taste

Steps

  1. Season and cook the meat until tender. Set aside with the stock.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot, add chopped onions, and fry lightly.
  3. Add the ground egusi and stir continuously until it thickens and forms lumps.
  4. Pour in meat stock and stir to dissolve the egusi paste into the soup.
  5. Add the cooked meat, fish, and ground crayfish. Stir well and simmer.
  6. Add chopped peppers and let it cook for about 10 minutes.
  7. Stir in the leafy greens and allow to cook for another 5 minutes.
  8. Adjust seasoning and serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or eba.
